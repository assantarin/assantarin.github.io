Negros Occidental is a province in the Philippines occupying the northwestern half of Negros Island abundant in both sugar and tourist spots. It is known as the Sugar Bowl of the Philippines for it produces more than half of the sugar output of the country. Aside from that, with the abundance of tourist spots like heritage sites, islands, sandbars, beach and mountain resorts, it is considered as one of the tourism hubs in Visayas.


It is the fourth most populated province in the Philippines, known for its Chicken Inasal Piaya, and Napoleones, but these are just a few of the famous foods that the city is known for. But more than these highly promoted delectable foods, there more cuisines that you should try when visiting the City of Smiles as manifested in various restaurants dotting the place. And is home to one of the Philippines' most active volcanoes; Mt. Kanlaon, also named Canlaon or Kanla-on.

Negos Occidental has many festivals, but the most well known among them are The Panaad Festival and The MassKara Festival. The Panaad Festival is a festival held annually during the month of April in Bacolod, the capital of Negros Occidental province in the Philippines, and The MassKara Festival of Bacolod City is one the biggest festivals in the Philippines.
